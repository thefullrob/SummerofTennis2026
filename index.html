Most days are shared on purpose: Ren can take Charlie's FH/serve reps as quality-ball work, and Charlie can take Ren's transition reps because it makes him less one-dimensional. They train together, but each player scores the rep against a different goal.

Three focus areas

  1. Hands at net — block-volley footwork. "Stop, then hit."
  2. Transition decisions — call out loud ("approach!" or "stay!") as moving.
  3. FH maintenance only — 20 min/session. Freed time goes to net + transition.

Practice structure

Every training day looks like this:

  • ⓪ Dynamic warmup — 6–7 min, off-court, BEFORE the practice clock starts. Light jog + leg swings + arm circles + lunges + high knees.
  • ① Hitting warmup — 20 min (first block of the 2-hr practice). Mini-tennis → half-court rally → baseline rally → serves + returns. Get timing, not heart rate.
  • ② Footwork circuit — 30 min. Built from 3–5 stations, never one drill for the whole block. A scheduled footwork drill becomes one station, then the app fills the rest with reaction, recovery, mirror, or transition footwork.
  • ③ Hitting drills — ~45–50 min. The focus drills for the day. Tap any drill name for full setup + execution.
  • ④ Competitive games — 20–25 min. At least two scored games every training day: serve + one, baseline direction, transition, pressure tiebreaks, or practice-set pieces.
  • ⑤ Cool-down — 10–15 min, AFTER the practice clock. Light walk + static stretches + foam roll + hydrate.

2-hour practice = hitting warmup + footwork + drills + games (120 min). Bookends ⓪ and cool-down are essential but not counted in the 2 hrs. Match days replace this structure entirely.
